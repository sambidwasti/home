*Postdoc researcher at CUA, working at NASA Goddard under CRESST2 cooperative agreement.*

I am part of the team to develop ComPair telescope, a prototype of AMEGO. AMEGO is a midex mission
concept. Additional information about [&lt;u&gt;AMEGO&lt;/u&gt;](https://asd.gsfc.nasa.gov/amego/). ComPair (and AMEGO) uses
four subsystem to achieve its scientific and instrument goals. These subsystems are Double Sided (DSSD) Silicon
Tracker, CzT calorimeter, CsI calorimeters and Anti-Coincidence detectors (ACD). ComPair went to beam test
(High Energy beam of particles) at [&lt;u&gt;TUNL&lt;/u&gt;](https://tunl.duke.edu/) (HIGS) facility at DUKE University in
May of 2022. The data from the beam test are being analyzed. Additionally, the instrument is being improved and
optimized in preparation for a balloon flight on fall of 2023.

### Compton Pair (ComPair) Instrument
#### Pipeline  
I am leading the [&lt;u&gt;Data Pipeline&lt;/u&gt;](https://mev-astronomy.gitlab.io/ComPairAPRA/pipeline/ComPair-unified-data/README_Sphinx.html)
group for the project. The pipeline includes the journey of the data from
its raw data stage (for each subsytem) to fully processed stage for additional analysis. The early stages of
the journey includes, applying energy and position calibration, various filters and threshold, at various
processing levels for each subsytem. The data is then combined and verfied via reconstructing the events using
[&lt;u&gt;MEGALIB Toolkit&lt;/u&gt;](https://megalibtoolkit.com/home.html).
#### Data Analysis and Simulation
Analyzing the data from calibration, beam test and future data from integration
testing and balloon flight. Data Analysis includes, but not limited to, looking at the spectra, image and various
statistics of the run. Simultaneously, simulating the instrument for the data runs (and its environment) to
compared the simulation to the real run which would reassure our understanding of the instrument.
#### Silicon Tracker 
Part of the silicon tracker hardware team where we assemble, calibrate and test the silicon
tracker layers. I am also part of the [&lt;u&gt;Tracker pipeline&lt;/u&gt;](https://mev-astronomy.gitlab.io/ComPairAPRA/tracker/ComPair-tracker-pipeline/)
team that focuses on the raw data and processing of the
raw data from the silicon tracker. In addition to the hardware, I am also responsible for building and maintaining
[&lt;u&gt;Tracker Interface&lt;/u&gt;](https://mev-astronomy.gitlab.io/ComPairAPRA/tracker/ComPair-tracker-interface/) which uses
plotly and dash in python.
